Simulation-Based Analytics for Quality-Associated Decision Support


A decision-support system for enhancing quality-associated decision-support processes by facilitating project quality performance forecasting, product quality performance measurement, product complexity analysis, operator quality performance assessment, and quality-induced rework man-hour estimation and control.

Computer-based quality management systems have been widely implemented throughout the construction industry as per the requirement of the International Organization for Standardization (ISO) 9000. Although these systems have facilitated the collection of vast amounts of quality management data, conversion of this data into usable information remains challenging for many practitioners. Automated, data-driven quality management systems, which facilitate the transformation of data into usable information, are often implemented to enhance decision-making processes. However, for a data-driven quality management system to be successful, it must accurately estimate process uncertainty. Integration of accurate, reliable, and straightforward approaches that measure uncertainty of inspection processes are instrumental for the successful implementation of automated, data-driven quality management systems.

This research has addressed these limitations by exploring and adapting Bayesian inference-based analytical solution and Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC)-based numerical solution for updating quality-associated decision-support systems in real-time. Using these accurate and reliable inputs, this research further develops novel, analytically-based approaches to improve the functionality of traditional pipe welding quality management systems.

Enhanced input modeling for construction simulation


In construction simulation, input models are typically represented as probabilistic distributions to account for the uncertain characteristics of construction operations. In todayâ€™s construction operations, various types of information are becoming available due to the implementation of advanced technologies (e.g., sensors and information systems) and these types of information have the potential to be dynamically incorporated for enhancing the reliability of input modeling.

The objective of this research is to enhance the state-of-the-art of simulation input modeling in a more dynamic, reliable, and informative manner through the use of artificial intelligence (AI) techniques. In detail, this research enhances input modeling through incorporating multi-source data to derive up-to-date and detailed input models, thereby, augmenting the decision-making processes in construction operations.

Modeling Public Sentiment on Infrastructure Services in Disasters: Social Media Mining Approach


The objective of this research is to model the public sentiment on infrastructure services to provide a rapid and human-centered metric to relief agencies for distributing resources through mining social media data.

Public sentiment, a direct indicator for representing public opinions and feelings, is an ideal candidate for providing consistent and human-centered insights on various infrastructure services to identify the most urgent ones for sustaining and saving human life. With advances in information technologies, social media is gaining increasing popularity for sharing opinions and feelings in disasters. Consequently, it becomes feasible and promising to model public sentiment in disasters through the collected opinions and feelings from social media due to their near real-time, social, and informative characteristics. Nevertheless, modeling of public sentiment is still limited due to the lack of systematic integration and development of infrastructure-specific algorithms.

This research aims to address these limitations by building a systematic data-mining pipeline, as well as developing critical algorithms for modeling public sentiment on infrastructure services in disasters.
